## 📝 摘要

This study proposes the development of an innovative microscopic laser system integrated with augmented reality (AR) technology for the real-time detection of residual tumour cells during surgical procedures. The system comprises a semiconductor laser diode emitting a focused beam onto the surgical area, where tumour-specific fluorescent markers bind to tumour cells, emitting fluorescence upon laser excitation. Augmented reality overlays digital information onto the user's view, providing real-time guidance and highlighting areas with residual tumour cells. A scanning mechanism ensures comprehensive examination, with the AR display facilitating interactive feedback. Sensitive photodetectors capture fluorescent signals for analysis, and a connected computer processes data to differentiate tumour cells from normal tissue. Safety features are incorporated to prevent tissue damage. This innovative design offers an intuitive and interactive interface, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of microscopic tumour cell detection, thereby potentially revolutionizing oncological surgeries.
