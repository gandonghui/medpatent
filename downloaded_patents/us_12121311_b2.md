# Patent: US-12121311-B2

## Title
Methods and systems for robotic-assisted surgery using customized bone registration guides

## Abstract
Described within are systems, methods and apparatus for a bone mounted robotic-assisted orthopedic surgery system for precise implant position, soft tissue balancing and guidance of tools during a surgical procedure, particularly partial or total knee replacement procedure. The system features a bone mounted robotic arm with end-effector for precise positioning of surgical tool, position in of implants and balancing of soft tissues. The reconfigurable robotic system requires minimal training by surgeons, is intuitive to use similar to conventional instrumented surgery and has a small footprint. The system works with existing, conventional instruments, patient specific instruments, sensor-assisted systems and computer-assisted systems and does not require increased surgical time and safely provides the enhanced precision achievable by robotic-assisted systems and computer-assisted technologies.

## Claims
I claim: 
     
       1. A surgical system comprising:
 a computing device comprising:
 one or more processors; 
 a communication interface receiving a plurality of two-dimensional images of a patient&#39;s joint the subject of a joint arthroplasty procedure; and 
 at least one memory storing non-transitory computer-readable instructions that, when executed by the one or more processors, cause the computing device to:
 generate, based on locating a plurality of mating shapes within the plurality of two-dimensional images of the patient&#39;s joint, one or more dimensions for a customized registration guide for the patient&#39;s joint for use during the joint arthroplasty procedure, the customized registration guide comprising one or more drill bit guides; 
 
 
 a plurality of registration pins configured to be mounted to the patient&#39;s anatomy based on the one or more drill bit guides of the customized registration guide; and 
 a robotic device comprising:
 a controller base unit configured to activate a first link joint of the controller base and a second link joint of the controller base, the second link joint distinct from the first link joint; 
 a mounting device configured to be mounted to the plurality of registration pins and mechanically coupled with the first link joint of the controller base unit via a plurality of configurable links comprising at least a translational link configured to translate with two degrees of freedom transversal to each other to orient the controller base unit in relation to the mounting device and corresponding to the joint arthroplasty procedure; and 
 a configurable resection guide mechanically coupled with the second link joint of the controller base unit and oriented to provide a resection guide for the joint arthroplasty procedure; 
 
 wherein the first link joint provides one degree of rotational freedom between the mounting device and the controller base unit and the second link joint provides one degree of rotational freedom between the resection guide and the controller base unit. 
 
     
     
       2. The surgical system of  claim 1 , wherein the plurality of configurable links further comprises:
 a rotational link mechanically coupled with the translational link and configured to rotate the mounting device in relation to a linear link. 
 
     
     
       3. The surgical system of  claim 1 , wherein the plurality of configurable links further comprises:
 a second rotational link in mechanical communication with the first rotational link and configured to rotate the mounting device in relation to the first rotational link. 
 
     
     
       4. The surgical system of  claim 1 , wherein the first link joint and the second link joint are each mechanically coupled with one of a plurality of activators. 
     
     
       5. The surgical system of  claim 4 , wherein the controller base unit further comprises:
 a communication port receiving instructions to control the plurality of activators according to the joint arthroplasty procedure. 
 
     
     
       6. The surgical system of  claim 4 , wherein the controller base unit further comprises:
 an input device receiving inputs to control the plurality of activators. 
 
     
     
       7. The surgical system of  claim 1 , wherein the joint arthroplasty procedure comprises an intra-operative, soft-tissue gap balancing for both static and dynamic motion of the patient&#39;s anatomy. 
     
     
       8. The surgical system of  claim 1 , wherein the robotic device is configurable with one of two-degrees, three-degrees, four-degrees, five-degrees, or six-degrees of freedom. 
     
     
       9. The surgical system of  claim 1  wherein the plurality of configurable links are detachable from the first link joint of the controller base unit while the mounting device is mounted to the patient&#39;s anatomy. 
     
     
       10. The surgical system of  claim 1 , wherein the plurality of two-dimensional images of the patient&#39;s anatomy comprises a plurality of magnetic-resonance images.

     TECHNICAL FIELD 
     Embodiments of the present disclosure generally relate to systems and methods for creating and utilizing customized arthroplasty registration guides in joint replacement procedures. More specifically, the present disclosure relates to methods for creating arthroplasty registration guides customized to a particular patient for use in robot-assisted surgical procedures that provide three-dimensional reference information of the patient to the surgical robot to orient a surgical plan for execution by the surgical robot, including bone-mounted surgical robots.
